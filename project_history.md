## Inspiration
As university students, we use the Barcelona metro nearly every day and feel really fond of trains as a mode of transport. We wanted to create a project that would make the daily commute and transportation of so many people a little bit more interesting and strengthen the pride that the citizens of our city have for the subway.
***
## What it does
It is a game that assigns you to a faction corresponding to a metro line and makes you fight other factions by using the metro. It works by giving you points every time you take a train, based on the number of stops you pass. At the end of the week the two factions with the most amount of overall points enter the high leagues and duke it out between them while the other ones stay in the normal league. Every week this fight for line hegemony repeats.
***
## How we built it
We used a work division approach. We divided the idea into GUI, backend, and frontend, and developed them simultaneously, keeping in mind all interdependencies and minimal necessary functionality.
***
## Challenges we ran into
We had to work with a previously unknown to us toolset. Our experience in with Javascript frameworks is still basic, so the development process also involved a lot of learning. The P2P architecture also presented itself as something new we needed to understand. Additionally, working in a team always comes with organizational hurdles that we had to solve before anything else.
***
## Accomplishments that we're proud of
Overall, the whole project is a triumph for us and we are really happy to have finished it. Also, all the user sprites are hand drawn!
***
## What we learned
We gained a good understanding of the Next.js framework as well as core P2P concepts. 
***
## What's next for MetroClanWars
